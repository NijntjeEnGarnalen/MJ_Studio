Garden Diaries
Publication Design, 2024

Garden Diaries grew out of my ongoing observation of the flowerbeds in my residential community. I documented plants across the seasons, recording how they shift in color, density, and presence over time. The publication is structured as five small booklets: plant imagery is screen printed on translucent tracing paper, while the inner pages print the ground—soil and pavement tiles—so the landscape reads in layers. A string binds the five booklets together, threading them into a seasonal timeline.

Alongside the plants, I also observed human behavior in the same space. These observations became the first booklet, produced through screen printing for its organic process and warmer, more tactile tone. Different colors are used to mark different kinds of actions and encounters. The booklet includes fragments from the COVID period, as well as chronological records sourced from Google Earth that document the garden's transformation.

Memory serves as a thread, stringing together past recollections. The chronological records come from Google Earth, documenting the transformation of the garden.

The binding repeats throughout—rope as a timeline, a physical cue that time is not only read but held. The project closes with a set of small everyday events, edited into a compact folded zine that is easy to carry and read.

Mabel Chen © 2026
